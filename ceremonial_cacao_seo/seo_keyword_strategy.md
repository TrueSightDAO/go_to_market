# SEO Keyword Positioning Strategy — Ceremonial Cacao (USA)

**Purpose:** Match category expectations and differentiate Agroverse on regeneration, traceability, and community.  
**Inputs:** `competitor_analysis.json`, `positioning_summary.md`, and Agroverse differentiators.

**Insights from full-site index (80 pages per competitor):**
- **Ceremonial Cacao (Ora):** Heavy use of "ceremonial cacao" in product titles (24 product pages), "organic" appears frequently, emphasis on origin (Belize, Colombia, Guatemala, Tanzania, Uganda), "regenerative" and "traceability" present.
- **Ma Cacao:** "regenerative" appears 3x, "ceremonial" 2x, "origin" 5x across 57 pages. Product pages emphasize "ceremony bar" and origin.
- **Four Visions:** Has ceremonial cacao category but broader plant medicine focus. "Ceremonial cacao" appears in category/product pages.
- **Bar and Cocoa:** Multi-brand retailer; "ceremonial cacao" collection exists. Emphasizes "single origin" and craft chocolate.

**Pattern:** Competitors consistently use "ceremonial cacao" + origin in product titles. "Organic" is common. "Regenerative" and "traceability" are differentiators we share.

## 3. Content briefs (alignment with existing site)

- **Homepage:** Keep title and meta focused on “Regenerating our Amazon rainforest” + “ceremonial cacao” and “cacao circles.” Ensure “ceremonial cacao USA” and “organic ceremonial cacao” appear in meta or first fold.
- **Product pages:** Each ceremonial product should have title/meta with product name + “ceremonial cacao,” “organic,” and origin (e.g. Brazil). Use `docs/PRODUCT_CREATION_CHECKLIST.md` for schema and URLs.
- **Blog / Cacao Journeys:** Use “ceremonial cacao,” “regenerative,” “traceability,” “single origin” in headings and intro where relevant.
- **Farms / Shipments:** Emphasize “single origin,” “farm traceability,” “ceremonial cacao [origin].”

## 4. Chocolate vocabulary (stakeholder feedback + near-term bars)

**Why “chocolate” matters:** Shoppers and search demand often say *chocolate* while Agroverse’s live catalog today is *cacao* (ceremonial paste, nibs, etc.). Using *chocolate* only where it is accurate avoids confusion and builds a bridge to **single-estate dark chocolate bars** when those SKUs launch.

**On-site rules (until bar PDPs are live):**

- **Primary money keywords** stay **ceremonial cacao / organic cacao / single origin cacao** on titles, H1s, and meta for products you actually sell—no “buy dark chocolate bar” on pages that are not bar PDPs.
- **Natural secondary copy** can connect the two: e.g. “single-estate cacao from the same farms that will become our dark chocolate bars,” or “from our farms to your cup—and soon, bean-to-bar dark chocolate.” This satisfies Linda-style feedback without implying bars are checkout-ready.
- **Coming soon (optional block):** One clear module—*not* a buy button—e.g. “Single-estate dark chocolate bars — notify me” or “In development from [farm names].” Keeps trust high vs. thin “chocolate” landing pages with no product.
- **Blog / Cacao Journeys:** Educational titles merge intents safely: “Ceremonial cacao vs chocolate,” “What single-origin dark chocolate means,” “Bean to bar from Brazilian farms.” These capture chocolate-curious traffic and funnel to cacao SKUs today.
- **Structured data:** Add `Product` / `Offer` schema only when a bar URL and price exist; use `WebPage` + clear copy for “coming soon” pages, not fake offers.

**SEO targeting posture:**

- **Now:** Win on ceremonial cacao + origin + regenerative; use chocolate terms in **supporting** headings and body where factually true (cacao is the ingredient of chocolate; same estates).
- **At bar launch:** Add dedicated bar category + PDPs; expand titles/meta to “single origin dark chocolate,” “organic dark chocolate bar,” etc., aligned to DataForSEO/GSC winners you’ve already tracked.
