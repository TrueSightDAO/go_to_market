## 1. Recommendation in one paragraph

Show up as Ken's guest with something real — **not** a retail activation, a booth, or a sales target. The synthesis of all four proposals: **lead with taste, resolve with provenance.** Hand someone a small cup of single-estate cacao (Kimi's "grounding ritual" for people who spend the day orchestrating agents); when they ask "what's this?", the answer is a **cryptographically verifiable supply chain** they can scan and check (Claude's "provenance is a dual-use problem"; DeepSeek's "Purple Cow at a defense summit"). Publicize it the way we always do: the artifact carries the message, one truesight.me methodology essay seeds the narrative beforehand, we capture permission rather than sell, **no full-list newsletter blast**, and the real story ships *after* — only if earned. No discounts, no urgency, no announcement genre.

## 2. The frame — synthesis of all four readings

Each model found a true facet. The finalized frame uses them in sequence, not in competition:

| Layer | Source | Role on the day |
|---|---|---|
| **Taste first** | Kimi (verdict guardrail #1) | The first move is always the cup. Oscar's buttery Bahia (smoother, default) or Paulo's smoky Pará (adventurous). Let the cacao do the talking. |
| **Then provenance** | Claude / DeepSeek | When they ask, the hook for *this* room: *"Every cup traces to a specific farm in the Amazon — not because we say so, because the ledger does."* Same verifiable-systems instinct they work with daily. |
| **Grounding ritual context** | Kimi / DeepSeek | The coherence between the two sponsor communities: ClawCamp teaches multi-agent orchestration; Agroverse is the human-in-the-loop beverage *and* the output of a supply chain those same agent patterns route from Bahia to SF. |

**Smallest viable audience** (Kimi + DeepSeek agree): the exhausted founder who hasn't eaten, the veteran builder who's been pitching since 9 AM, the ClawCamp attendee who just shipped their first multi-agent workflow and needs five minutes of embodied calm. Serve them well; ignore everyone else. The goal is **one remarkable, verifiable moment**, not conversion.

## 3. What we do NOT do *(Kimi §5 — adopt verbatim as Gary's pre-event checklist)*

- **No demo booth, no branded backdrop, no pitch deck.** A bowl of cacao on a side table with hot water and QR codes is more Agroverse than a 6-foot vendor table. *(Claude, Kimi, DeepSeek all agree.)*
- **No full-list newsletter blast** (see §7). *(Claude + Kimi; OpenAI revised to agree.)*
- **No promotional discount codes / manufactured urgency.** No "tickets now," no "X% off." Breaks the curatorial register.
- **No QR-code checkout / payment demo.** These are one-time drinkers, not retail buyers. A payment QR feels extractive.
- **No Expo-West-style sampling pressure.** Quality of interaction over volume of handouts.
- **No cold blast to the attendee list.** It is not ours to scrape; permission comes through the opt-in QR. *(Claude / OpenAI.)*

> **Operating principle (Kimi):** *the risk of overdoing this (booth, banners, blast) is higher than the risk of underdoing it. Err on the side of minimal.*

## 4. On-the-day execution

### 4.1 Placement — ambient, at natural pause moments *(Kimi §4.1)*

No booth. Position cacao as the break beverage where people already pause:

| Summit moment | Cacao role |
|---|---|
| Morning welcome | Alternative to coffee; sets a different tone for the day |
| Networking / demo breaks | Reset between conversations; cacao as conversation starter |
| ClawCamp workshop block | "This is what we drink before we build" — coordinate with ClawCamp hosts to serve at session start |
| Closing reception | Wind-down; a slow exit instead of rushed goodbyes |

### 4.2 Format *(Kimi §4.2 + DeepSeek §4.1)*
- **Serve hot, small cups (4–6 oz).** A tasting, not a meal. Pre-grated cacao in a dispenser → no prep friction (DeepSeek).
- **Both origins available** — default Oscar's (more accessible), offer Paulo's as the adventurous option (Kimi).
- **Molinillo / whisk** — the preparation is part of the ritual (Kimi).
- **3–5 retail bags (200g) on display** with QR visible, so people can handle the packaging and scan (DeepSeek §4.1).

### 4.3 The one-line story Gary says when asked "what's this?" *(DeepSeek §4.3 — punchy and accurate)*

> "Ceremonial cacao from Bahia, Brazil. Single-estate, named after the farmer who runs it. Each bag plants a tree — the QR code shows you exactly which one. The supply chain runs on AI agents we built."

If they ask about the AI agents: *"Same pattern they're teaching in the ClawCamp room."* A fact with an invitation to scan — not a pitch.

**Tree-planting framing — political-literacy guardrail (Kimi, via `RETAILER_ONBOARDING_PLAYBOOK.md` §14, Brasil Kiss lesson):** lead with indigenous inclusion and land restoration in the Xingu corridor, **not** a raw "plant a tree" line.

### 4.4 QR strategy — two codes, one durable destination

| QR | Destination | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Provenance / traceability** | The serialized bag's tree-planting proof page (`compiled_` URL from the Agroverse QR batch) | Claude / DeepSeek |
| **Permission / opt-in** | A page **we control** at `agroverse.shop` (see §6.2), with newsletter signup + "interested in carrying cacao?" checkbox | Claude (key decision #1) |

- **Serve the opt-in landing from our own repo, not a third-party Google Form** — durable, survives the event, deployable via existing GitHub Pages flow (Claude key decision #1).
- **Don't build a new signup form — extend the existing one** so signups route through the same pipe to the Main Ledger `Agroverse News Letter Subscribers` tab (Claude key decision #2).
- **Tracking:** event-unique batch code `DTS-2026-06-26` + `utm_source=event&utm_medium=qr&utm_campaign=dts-20260626` for quantitative scan attribution (OpenAI).

## 5. Pre-event — site

### 5.1 truesight.me — the pre-event asset *(Claude; endorsed by Kimi verdict A.3)*
Publish a **methodology essay before the event** — *"provenance is a dual-use problem,"* demonstrated with cacao. Right surface (ops/methodology, not consumer), right timing (seeds the narrative so attendees can read before they scan), right register (plumbing-and-soul, quiet contrarianism, byline `by Gary Teh, long time contributor` or `by Claude (Anthropic)`). Working titles: *"Provenance is a dual-use problem"* / *"You can taste this supply chain — and verify it."*

### 5.2 agroverse.shop — discoverable, not promoted
- **Use the existing `event-details-registration/` pattern** (Kimi §6.1 — precedent: `cacao-circle-at-orbis86-ai-x-web3-in-gaming-happy-hour-gdc`). Create `event-details-registration/dual-tech-summit-2026/index.html`: date, place, who's pouring, farms featured, footer mantra. **No RSVP funnel.**
- This page (or a `cacao-journeys/dual-tech-summit-2026/`) is also the **opt-in QR destination** (Claude §4.4) — so a scan lands on a *named place + farm story*, not a bare form.
- **SEO on that page only:** title/description, OpenGraph/Twitter image, canonical URL, Schema.org `Event` block with `offers` as free tasting (OpenAI). Update `sitemap.xml`; verify OG preview.
- **Optional:** a single quiet, place-anchored line on `/wholesale` near the stockist list — *"June 2026 — San Francisco: pouring at the Dual Tech Summit."* No exclamation, no banner (Claude §4.5a).
- **What we do NOT do:** homepage hero banner, site-wide announcement bar, countdown timer, pre-event agroverse blog post *(consensus; against OpenAI's original draft)*.

### 5.3 Pre-event dry run — the highest-leverage step *(Claude key decision #3; singled out by DeepSeek §11.3 and §11.5)*
**One week before the event:** print a test QR, scan it, verify the signup lands in `Agroverse News Letter Subscribers`, confirm `send_newsletter.py` can address the new subscriber. Catch the dead link / CORS / sheet-permission error **before 200 people scan it.** This single action prevents the one failure mode that kills the whole activation.

## 6. Pre-event — newsletter *(consensus: NO full-list blast)*

A pre-event "come to a defense summit" send to the wellness list is **wrong-audience + announcement genre** (`AGROVERSE_NEWSLETTER_WORKFLOW.md` §7). The full-list newsletter moment is **post-event**, if earned. Pre-event, in order of preference:

1. **Personal 1:1 notes (preferred)** — Gary to specific in-network people who'd want to be *in that room* (founders, investors, crypto/DAO/defense-curious). Warm, specific, one human to another. *(Claude + Kimi; the workflow carves the personal network out of group blasts.)*
2. **Segmented / local touch** — if we can isolate SF-local or tech/DAO-curious subscribers, a small story-framed send respects anticipated/personal/relevant. Not the whole list. *(Claude; OpenAI revised to "segmented/local only, no T-2 resend.")*
3. **Piggyback** — if a newsletter is already scheduled for a real reason, a one-line P.S. + link to the truesight essay is fine. Don't spin up a dedicated send. *(Kimi.)*
4. **Bay Area Partnered stores & Gary's personal contacts** — direct individual messages, never a group blast (Kimi §6.2).
5. **ClawCamp's own channels** — a brief mention where co-sponsor communities are already permissioned (Kimi).

**If no newsletter is scheduled and we can't segment cleanly:** skip the pre-event newsletter entirely. Silence here is consistent with the register.

## 7. Post-event loop

- **One field-dispatch newsletter, 3–5 days after** *(Kimi §7.1)* — lead with a specific human moment (a veteran founder tasting Paulo's smoky Pará for the first time), one grounded taste note, brief ClawCamp/ritual connection, forward gesture, commerce as a quiet link. Standard pipeline: draft → review (Kirsten + Fatima) → send with tracking (`AGROVERSE_NEWSLETTER_WORKFLOW.md` §4).
- **Hit List follow-up** *(Kimi §7.2 / Claude §5.2)* — anyone who expressed retail interest gets a row: `Status: Research`/`Shortlisted`, `Source: Dual Tech Summit 2026`, Notes (what they tasted, what they said). Enters the standard warm-up pipeline; **Grok** generates the follow-up drafts via `suggest_manager_followup_drafts.py`.
- **DApp Remark** *(Kimi §7.3)* — log the activation (date, servings, farms served, notable conversations, what landed/didn't) as training data for the next event.
- **Update the event page to past-tense** *(Kimi §7.4)* — *"This event has passed. Thank you for being part of our community!"* — matching every other past event page.
- **Qualitative loop** *(DeepSeek §11.8)* — update `OUTREACH_QUALITATIVE_LOOP.md` with new objection patterns, conversion signals, or taste feedback surfaced at the event.
- **truesight.me essay (post-event option)** — if the day produced a real observation, a methodology essay (not a recap). Gary's call whether Claude or Kimi drafts it — no scheduling pressure (DeepSeek §11.6).
