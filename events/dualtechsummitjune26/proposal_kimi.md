# Dual Tech Summit 2026 — Cacao Activation Proposal

**Event:** SVH Capital Presents: Dual Tech Summit 2026  
**Date:** June 26, 2026  
**Venue:** American Legion San Francisco Post 1 and Post 599  
**Audience:** Veteran founders, defense-tech operators, dual-use investors, ClawCamp builders  
**Our role:** Sponsor (Agroverse + ClawCamp co-listed)  
**Offer:** Ken (SVH Capital) invited us to make ceremonial cacao available throughout the summit  

---

## 1. What this is (and what it isn't)

This is not a retail activation. We are not there to consign product, demo QR checkout, or hit a sales target. We are there because a partner who knows our work asked us to show up with something real — and because the people in that room are building the next layer of technological infrastructure, often without any infrastructure for themselves.

The frame is simple: **ceremonial cacao as a grounding ritual for people who spend their days orchestrating agents.**

ClawCamp runs workshops on personal orchestration and multi-agent workflows. Agroverse supplies the drink that keeps the humans in the loop... human. The narrative isn't product placement — it's coherence between two sponsor communities.

---

## 2. Audience reality check

These are not our typical retail partners. They don't walk into apothecaries looking for ceremonial cacao. Many will never have tasted it. Some will confuse it with hot chocolate. A few will be skeptical of anything that sounds like "wellness."

That's fine. The goal isn't conversion to retail partner. The goal is a single remarkable experience — a taste, a story, a moment of slowness inside a high-velocity event — that a subset of them will remember and, eventually, remark on.

**Smallest viable audience:** The exhausted founder who hasn't eaten properly, the veteran builder who's been pitching since 9 AM, the ClawCamp attendee who just debugged their first multi-agent workflow and needs five minutes of embodied calm before the next session. Serve them well. Ignore everyone else.

---

## 3. The story we tell (farm-first, translated)

Our standard retail pitch leads with single-estate traceability, regenerative harvest, and the farmer's name. That still works here — but the hook needs translation for a crowd that understands cryptographic verification better than they understand terroir.

**The opener:**

> "Every cup traces back to a specific farm in the Brazilian Amazon — Oscar's Farm in Bahia, or Paulo's Farm in Pará. Not because we say so. Because the ledger does."

Then: taste. Let the cacao do the talking. If they want more, tell them about the RSA-signed provenance events, the QR-coded traceability, the fact that the same infrastructure they use for smart contracts is what guarantees this farmer got paid. The plumbing-and-soul register holds.

**Taste profile language (reuse from field playbook):**
- Oscar's Farm (Bahia) — deep European chocolate, buttery, smooth
- Paulo's Farm (Pará) — smoky, earthy, unfolding into floral

If they ask about the tree-planting angle: lead with indigenous inclusion and land restoration in the Xingu corridor, not the raw "plant a tree" line. See `RETAILER_ONBOARDING_PLAYBOOK.md` §14 (Brasil Kiss lesson) for the political-literacy pitfall.

## 4. Execution — light and contextual

### 4.1 Placement

No booth. No banner. Position cacao as the ambient break beverage at natural pause moments:

| Summit moment | Cacao role |
|---------------|-----------|
| 09:00 AM Welcome | Morning offering — alternative to coffee, sets a different tone for the day |
| 11:00 AM Networking & Demos | Reset between conversations; cacao as conversation starter |
| 02:00 PM Deep-Dive Sessions | Afternoon grounding; ClawCamp attendees especially |
| 05:00 PM Closing Reception | Wind-down; slow exit instead of rushed goodbyes |

If there is a dedicated ClawCamp workshop block (11 AM – 4 PM), coordinate with the ClawCamp hosts to serve cacao at the start of the session. The framing: "This is what we drink before we build."

### 4.2 Preparation & format

- **Serve hot, in small cups (4–6 oz).** This is a tasting, not a meal replacement.
- **Prepare both origins.** Let people choose, or default to Oscar's (smoother, more accessible) and offer Paulo's as the "adventurous" option.
- **Minimal signage.** One small card per cup with the farm name, region, and a QR link to `agroverse.shop/farms/<farm>/`. No price. No "buy now."
- **No checkout, no sales pitch.** If someone asks where to get it, hand them a card or point them to the site. If they own a shop and want to talk retail, take their email and add them to the Hit List with a note: *"Met at Dual Tech Summit 2026 — tasted [farm], expressed interest in retail."*

### 4.3 Materials needed

| Item | Source | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| Ceremonial cacao paste (Oscar's Farm, Bahia) | Existing inventory / Kirsten's stock | Estimate 100–150 servings |
| Cacao paste (Paulo's Farm, Pará) | Existing inventory | 50–75 servings for variety |
| Hot water / portable kettle | Venue or bring | Confirm with American Legion Post kitchen access |
| Small compostable cups | Stock or quick order | 4–6 oz |
| Wooden whisk (molinillo) or small frother | Agroverse kit | The preparation is part of the ritual |
| Farm cards (small, minimal) | Print locally or at home | Farm name, region, QR to farm page |
| Business cards | Existing stock | For retail leads only |

## 5. What we don't do

These are deliberate omissions, consistent with our operating conventions:

- **No newsletter blast before the event.** Our cadence rule (`AGROVERSE_NEWSLETTER_WORKFLOW.md` §7): *"Only email when there is a real new thing to say."* The real new thing is the story we gather, not the fact that we're attending.
- **No promotional discount codes.** Manufacturing urgency breaks the curatorial register.
- **No QR-code checkout demo.** These are one-time drinkers, not retail partners. A payment QR would feel extractive.
- **No "Agroverse booth" or branded backdrop.** We're a sponsor showing up with a beverage, not a vendor extracting leads.
- **No Expo-West-style sampling pressure.** This is a single-day summit, not a three-day trade show. Quality of interaction over volume of handouts.

## 7. Post-event narrative

The real publicization happens after, not before.

### 7.1 One newsletter dispatch

Send one email to the `Agroverse News Letter Subscribers` list within 3–5 days after the event. Frame it as a field dispatch, not an announcement.

- Lead with a specific human moment (e.g., a veteran founder's reaction to tasting Paulo's smoky Pará for the first time)
- Include one taste-profile note grounded in the actual servings
- Mention the ClawCamp workshop context briefly — the connection between agent-building and ritual
- End with a forward gesture (visit a farm page, check out ClawCamp's next session)
- Commerce lives at the bottom as a quiet link to `agroverse.shop`

Follow standard newsletter workflow: draft → review (Kirsten + Fatima) → send with tracking. Reference `AGROVERSE_NEWSLETTER_WORKFLOW.md` §4 for the full pipeline.

### 7.2 Hit List follow-up

Any attendee who expressed retail interest gets a Hit List row with:
- `Status`: `Research` or `Shortlisted`
- `Source`: `Dual Tech Summit 2026`
- `Notes`: What they tasted, what they said, next action if any

They then enter the standard AI warm-up pipeline (`RETAILER_ONBOARDING_PLAYBOOK.md` §3) or manual field-visit track as appropriate.

### 7.3 DApp Remark

Log the activation as a `DApp Remark` tied to the venue (American Legion Post 1/599) or as a standalone event entry. Include: date, servings prepared, farms served, notable conversations, what landed, what didn't. This becomes training data for the next event activation.

### 7.4 Update the event page

After the event, update `event-details-registration/dual-tech-summit-2026/index.html` to replace any active language with the past-tense marker:

> *"This event has passed. Thank you for being part of our community!"*

This matches the pattern used on all other past event pages and keeps the site honest.

## 10. Decision

**Recommended: Yes, with the light frame described above.**

We show up with cacao, serve it quietly at natural break moments, tell the farm story when asked, and walk away with one good newsletter dispatch and a handful of retail leads. No blast. No booth. No pressure. Just a sponsor bringing something real to a room full of builders.

The risk of overdoing it (booth, banners, sales pitch) is higher than the risk of underdoing it. Err on the side of minimal.

## Appendix A — Comparative verdict: who should execute this

Four proposals were drafted for this activation: Kimi (this doc), Claude, OpenAI, and DeepSeek. Below is the comparative assessment used to decide who handles execution.

### A.3 The decisive differences

**1. Narrative sharpness for this specific room**

Claude is the only one who found the angle that is *remarkable to this audience*. "You can taste this supply chain — and verify it" lands with defense-tech founders in a way that "one cup → one tree" (OpenAI) or "grounding ritual" (Kimi) does not. The Purple Cow here isn't the cacao itself — it's the fact that a roomful of people who build verifiable systems can hold a physical product whose provenance is cryptographically guaranteed by the same infrastructure patterns they work with daily.

**2. Pre-event asset on the correct surface**

Claude proposes a truesight.me methodology essay *before* the event. This is the right surface (truesight.me for ops/methodology essays, not agroverse.shop for consumer stories), the right timing (seeds the narrative so attendees can read it before scanning the QR), and the right register (plumbing-and-soul, quiet contrarianism). The other proposals either skip pre-event content entirely (Kimi), push it to the wrong surface (OpenAI's social posts and homepage banner), or make it too thin (DeepSeek's wholesale one-liner).

**3. Respect for conventions without paralysis**

OpenAI is too aggressive — full-list blasts, homepage banners, manufactured urgency. DeepSeek sends a pre-event newsletter blast to the full wellness list, which is both wrong-audience and a cadence violation. Kimi (this doc) is the most convention-compliant but risks being *too* quiet — a whisper that nobody hears. Claude threads the needle: no blast, no banner, but a strong essay that gives the event narrative weight before the day.
